IP/00/1329

Bruxelles, 21 novembre 2000

Euro-trofei Il commissario Solbes consegna il premio alle cinque imprese vincintrici

Obiettivo degli Euro-trofei è premiare quelle imprese e i loro partner commerciali che hanno saputo sviluppare approcci innovativi per prepararsi all'euro e trarre vantaggio dalle opportunità strategiche offerte dalla moneta unica. Pedro Solbes, commissario europeo per gli affari economici e finanziari, ha dichiarato nel consegnare i trofei: "Queste pratiche esemplari, se ampiamente diffuse, potranno aiutare altre PMI ad attuare il loro progetto euro. A 14 mesi dall' "€-day", la Commissione intensifica gli sforzi per promuovere tali iniziative". Per l'impresa premiata, l'eurotrofeo è il riconoscimento del suo esemplare operato e costituisce un eccezionale fattore di valorizzazione agli occhi della clientela e dei collaboratori.

Cura l'organizzazione degli Euro-trofei l'Associazione per l'unione monetaria dell'Europa (AUME) che opera grazie al sostegno della Commissione europea e in collaborazione con la Federazione europea degli esperti contabili e IBM.

Obiettivo degli Euro-trofei è premiare le imprese che vantano una strategia esemplare di passaggio alla moneta unica, per poi utilizzarne l'esperienza come sprone a aiuto alle altre imprese. In altri termini, gli Euro-trofei intendono sensibilizzare le PMI europee ai benefici di una buona preparazione all'euro e individuare, nel contempo, le buone pratiche che possano aiutarle in tal senso. Sono ancora molte, infatti, le PMI che non hanno preso sul serio il passaggio all'euro e che tendono a rimandare i preparativi all'ultimo momento. In tal modo, rischiano di dover agire precipitosamente a fine 2001 e di perdere i vantaggi legati ad una buona preparazione dal punto di vista dell'amministrazione interna, commerciale e finanziario.

Migliaia di imprese si sono interessate all'operazione attraverso il sito Internet creato per l'occasione. Su un totale di 257 candidature, 67 vengono dalla Francia, 31 dalla Germania, 36 dai Paesi Bassi e 123 dagli altri paesi. Il rilevante numero di candidati di questi tre paesi è dovuto al fatto che sono state già realizzate operazioni analoghe a livello nazionale.

20 delle 79 candidature inizialmente selezionate sono state presentate alla giuria composta da rappresentanti della Commissione europea, dell'Associazione per l'unione monetaria dell'Europa, della Federazione europea degli esperti contabili e di IBM. La giuria si è riunita il 9 novembre a Bruxelles per decidere le imprese da premiare. In base al regolamento dell'operazione, sono state prescelte cinque PMI, ovvero una per categoria: industria, commercio, turismo, trasporti e servizi e artigianato e imprese individuali.

Le imprese vincitrici hanno ricevuto un premio più un computer portatile. Sono stati altresì premiati i professionisti che hanno assistito tali imprese nei preparativi all'euro.

Advisors who assisted the company in the changeover to the euro

Mr. D. A. de Boom, ABN AMRO

Comments on the company's changeover to the euro

BEN VROKLAGE FASHION bv has a women's and men's clothing store that focuses on the middle and upper segments. It operates with an advanced loyalty system, through which its over four thousand customers can automatically earn a bonus discount. The customer receives a 5% bonus on each purchase made in the store.

This is done through a plastic customer card and an electronic cash register. Every six months the customer receives a cheque stating the total bonus amount earned. This amount is deducted from the next purchase.

Starting in 2001, the customer cards and bonus cheques will all be stated in euros. This is well prepared, and the cards and cheques have a pleasant look. Customers will then receive their euro bonus cheques every two months. They can save these and, after the introduction of euro currency, deduct them from their future purchases.

But there is more. All receipts and price tags will likewise show euro amounts as from the first day of 2001. In December 2000 all customers will receive a letter with information about this bonus action. Plastic bags and envelopes will both be issued in an euro format with euro notes and coins. The display windows will feature the euro promotion year, and the local press will be kept informed. All in all, this is a perfect way to have both customers and employees get used to the new currency and for the store to get itself into the picture.

The cash registers and PIN equipment have already been made euro-proof. BEN VROKLAGE FASHION thus avoids higher costs, and payment delays in the future.

In getting ready for the euro, this company excels in what can already be achieved today in those business sectors that primarily deal with customers who pay in cash for the products and services bought.

FIGI, THE NETHERLANDS

General characteristics of the company

FIGI is active in hotel trade, restaurants, banqueting, theatre, cinema, and tourism.

They employ 140 people and their annual turnover is 10 millions euros

General Manager : Mr. Jos P. M. Ruijs

Het Rond, 2

3701 Hs Zeist (near Utrecht)

E-mail: jruijs@figi.nl

Tel : +31 30 692 74 92

Advisor who assisted the company in the changeover to the euro

Mr. Eugene Van Zijl, Deloitte & Touche and Mr. Hans Telkamp, Rabobank.

Comments on the company's changeover to the euro

As early as 1996 FIGI started to work with the euro by means of double pricing based on the expected future euro value. Significant public interest was shown and represented by two major questions: "What is the value of the euro?" and "Is it already possible to pay with the euro?" This led to the introduction of its own virtual euros early 1997. At that moment FIGI was the first anywere to accept the euro as a means of payment.

The strategy develloped resulted in extra revenues and much publicity both in the Netherlands and abroad.

On top of that, FIGI took hold of every opportunity to inform other companies about its experiences with the euro, particularly by participating in seminars and broadcasts. In 1998 as a result of FIGI's efforts, the Golden Tulip chain in Holland also started to use the euro for vouchers, and as a interesting communication tool towards the public. FIGI has always seen cooperation with others as most effective, thereby on a smaller scale - taking benifit of the essence of the reason behind the introduction of the euro; integration and cooperation.

They will keep on supplying business-to-business training facilities in 2001, while continuing the efforts to communicatie with the public. In this way they are playing a major role in making other companies as well as the publice wake up to the reality of the euro: " this has been very stimulating for our personnel says Jos Ruijs, as they like to participate in a process of innovation."

FIGI has opted for a step-by-step introduction of the euro. In its restaurant prices are stated in euros. The bill is likewise prepared in euros, with a conversion of the total amount into guilders. Restaurant visitors are used to rounding of the amount at the time of payment by giving tips. Implementing the euro does not therefore lead to any delay. In the cinema, however, tips are exceptional. To avoid unnecessary delays upon payment, it has therefore been decided here to maintain prices in guilders for the time being.Also on the purchase side FIGI takes advantage of the changeover to the euro. It expects to realise savings by being able to make proper price comparisons of suppliers on an international basis.The training of staff for the introduction of the euro was easier to achieve as they were already exposed to the use of the euro within the company.

In the final week of 2001, FIGI will celebrate its fifth euro anniversary by organising a conference about the marketing opportunities of the euro, especially within the tourist- and entertainment businesses. FIGI will then have the benefit of five years of euro experience; an experience unique in Europe, but shared with many others through the many media presentations as well as contributions to seminars, among them an advisorship for the European Commission in their 1998 conference on the future of the euro within the tourist industry.

CITY SENSORS SA, FRANCE

General characteristics of the company

CITY SENSORS SA is an industrial production company. It works with cutting-edge technology in the domain of industrial captors (pressure, gas and liquid captors).

The company employs four people and has a turnover of 1 million euros, of which 30% is in exports.

Chairman : Mr. Jean-Michel DARMON

15 Rue du Buisson aux fraises, ZI de La Bonde

91741 MASSY

E-mail: jmd.city@net-clic.net

Tel : +33 1 64 47 15 55

Advisors who assisted the company in the changeover to the euro

Ms. Virginie TISSERAND, Société Générale

Ms. Laurence GASIGLIA, BICS Banque Populaire

Comments on the company's changeover to the euro

Sales

The euro has allowed CITY SENSORS to energize its high-class image, an improvement favourably appreciated by its clients. CITY SENSORS's early changeover to the euro has permitted the company to now devote all its efforts to other problematic areas, and in particular, as they say, "to increase its commercial efforts, thus giving the company a head-start on its competitors." Its "clients are now completely familiar with billing in euros."

Purchases

The principal supplier of CITY SENSORS has also changed over to the euro, thus simplifying the company's purchase policy. In fact, working in euros is advantageous for both companies "for the obvious reason that they avoid the exchange fees imposed by the banks."

Finances

The euro has opened up advantageous banking opportunities for CITY SENSORS. "In February 1999, the company opened a second account in euros so as to generate competition between the banks, and was thus able to take advantage of better tariffs."

Back office operations

"Since January 1, 2000, all the bills, both within France and in export, are in euros." Today, there is only one option for clients and suppliers, with the result that the administration, both of credits and debits, is noticeably simplified."

Since the beginning of 1998, the personnel had been entirely familiarized with the euro in order to change over the company's accountancy to the euro as of January 1999. The employees' salary is also paid in euros."

Information technology

The changeover to the euro has rendered indispensable the replacement of out-dated computer hardware.

Overall Competitiveness

Since CITY SENSOR has completed its transition process as regards the euro, it is now able to"actively and intensively concentrate its efforts on commercialization, while other companies are only starting to wonder about the euro. This gives the company a considerable advantage."

SCHWINGEL AUTOMATEN GMBH, GERMANY

General characteristics of the company

SCHWINGEL AUTOMATEN is a service company which sets up vending machines.

They employ 30 people and their annual turnover is 3,3 millions euro.

President: Mr. Franz Einhaus

Glück Auf Str. 384

46284 Dorsten

Germany

E-mail: 04@t-online.de

Tel: + 49 23 627 14 39

Advisors who assisted the company in the changeover to the euro

Ms. Silvia Besse, ZENIT GmbH / Euro Info Center

Mr. Karl Besse, Bundesverband Automatenunternehmer E.V.

Comments on the company's changeover to the euro

Sales

They say: "Our company sets up vending machines and we therefore do not sell goods in the proper sense. We believe that our clients' satisfaction increases by changing over swiftly (to the euro) and by giving full information in due time. This will attract their interest for the euro."

Purchases

"By taking stock of all appliances and checking them on their euro-compatibility, we were able to decide very soon, which appliances were not adaptable. Therefore we could get rid of them soon. When purchasing new machines or appliances, top priority is given to the euro-compatibility. By purchasing euro compatible appliances at an early stage we saved a lot of money and avoided bad investments. Consequently, we could spread investments over a long period and ensure the liquidity of the company."

Human resources

"We informed all employees at an early stage of the euro and assured that they would be involved in the preparations. For example, we set up a "euro team" that works on the tasks of the project checklist. They employees informed and trained themselves in the field of technology and accounting. So we could soon choose and introduce euro-compatible software for the administration of the appliances. All employees are highly motivated as they recognised that the future of the company is depending on a successful changeover to the euro."

Finances

"The capital management has not yet been simplified. Our company needs a lot of cash money and we work closely with our banks in order to change DEM against euro notes and coins as smoothly as possible. Therefore we inform these business partners regularly. We established a detailed action plan, including logistics."

Back office operations

"Measures of rationalisation did not take place. The euro preparation did rather result in additional work. It is mainly the new software that facilitates work."

Information technology

After analysing how the euro affects our company, we had clear criteria for the selection of new software. We postponed this decision before. We also purchased new hardware on this occasion. Due to the new system the working processes and the controlling have been simplified.

Overall Competitiveness

As we started dealing with the euro at an early stage, we could demonstrate our business partners, particularly banks, suppliers, competitors and clients being a competent partner. Through our systematic work we gained confidence when changing over to the euro. We learned how to do project management. The business press reported several times about our preparations to the changover; we thus gained competence and respect.

Our experience (project plan, check list, project management) had been summarised in a changeover guide for all companies setting up vending machines (Editor: Bundesverband Automatenunternehmer e. V., Bonn, author: Euro Info Center ZENIT GmbH, Mülheim).

NEWMEC SRL, ITALY

General characteristics of the company

NEWMEC manufactures microprocessors.

They employ 20 people and their annual turnover is 2 millions euro.

Chief Executive: Mrs. Susanna Spirolazzi

Via Beato Angelico, 21

20052 Monza Mi

Italy

E-mail: Newmec@aladata.it

Tel: + 39 0 392 024 452

Advisors who assisted the company in the changeover to the euro

Ms Viviana Bianchi, and Ms. Francesca Passamonti, BANCA INTESA.

Comments on the company's changeover to the euro

Sales

"They will be facilitated throughout Europe when all our customers are ready to accept the euro. All our international banking operations are in euros."

Purchases

NEWMEC says: "with the euro we have eliminated the exchange risk and reduced banking charges for the payment of European suppliers"… "Since January 1999, we price and invoice in euro throughout Europe, but our Italian business partners keep on using the lira. Consequently we are often obliged to execute double work and to keep a dual pricing."

Finances

Since January 1999, NEWMEC is using the euro in all its transactions (invoices, payments): "but unfortunately, with Italian partners, if we issue documents in euro and lira, they still use Italian currency". NEWMEC regrets that all their partners are not ready, as they have to execute double work during the transition period.

Back office operations

The company still encounters difficulties with a number of Italian public services which refuse that declarations be filled in euro (eg; Import/export documents)

Information technology

" Our microprocessors are already qualified, so we won't have trouble when the euro is definitively used as a sole currency "

Overall Competitiveness

The company is considered by its partners as a "vanguard company, technologically and administratively", and they hope to reap the benefits of their preparation in the near future: "One day, people will be persuaded that a single European currency will be a benefit for working time, for accounting, for the transparency of prices, for international trade and for European competitiveness".
