Ecopoints: majority agreement reached

On 21 September 2000 the Council agreed, by majority vote, on a compromise solution on ecopoints. This should make it possible to share out traffic fairly within the legal framework created by Protocol No 9 of the Act of Accession of Austria. Loyola de Palacio, the Vice-President responsible for transport and energy, welcomed this agreement: "I believe that common sense has prevailed and that this wise decision by the Council will open the way to striking a balance between economic needs and environmental protection." She added: "Now we must evaluate the results of the ecopoint system to see what improvements could be made in the future to maintain freedom of movement and at the same time protect the environment in this vulnerable region."

The Council adopted this compromise by written procedure at midnight on 21 September 2000. First, the Council voted unanimously to initiate this written procedure. Next the proposal was put to a majority vote. Austria voted against the agreement, Belgium abstained and the other 13 Member States voted in favour.

The compromise covers the following points:

    1. Agreement on the number of ecopoints to be distributed by the Commission for 2000, following revision by Austria of the statistics on which the calculation was based (a total reduction of 1 009 478 ecopoints compared with the number that should have been granted this year had traffic not exceeded the maximum threshold).

    2. Spreading of the reduction over 4 years (30%/30%/30%/10%).

3. Retention of the 108% threshold for the year 2000.

    4. Commitment by the Commission to pay particular attention to the case of Greece when distributing the reserve.

    5. Commitment by the Commission to report on the ecopoints system by the end of 2000 and, in particular, to examine issues such as application of the 108% clause, statistics, "cowboy" operators, etc.

Figures showing the distribution of the ecopoints are set out in the Annex.

Background:

For the record, Protocol No 9 of the Act of Accession of Austria to the Community permits application of the ecopoint system, with the aim of limiting the number of lorries transiting Austria within a Community framework. Lorries must "pay" a number of ecopoints proportional to the amount of pollution which they cause. The total number of ecopoints available to the Member States is set annually and reduced every year with a target of reducing pollution by 60% over 12 years. However, Protocol No 9 adds a second reduction mechanism, which is activated if the number of transit journeys in any one year exceeds the total in the base year by more than 8%. Since this happened in 1999, the Commission was required to apply this reduction mechanism.

However, if the entire reduction had been applied solely in 2000 this would have had the disproportionate effect of preventing almost any transit traffic through Austria for the rest of this year. Measures therefore had to be taken to keep goods moving freely and the single market fully operational. To achieve this, the reduction in the number of ecopoints has been spread over four years.
