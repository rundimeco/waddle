IP/05/419

Brussell, 12 ta' April 2005

Il-Kummissjoni tniedi programm ta’ ħidma ta’ ħames snin biex issaħħaħ is-Sħubija Ewro-Mediterranja

Il-Kummissjoni Ewropea llum ħabbret programm ta’ ħidma wiesgħa biex issaħħaħ is-Sħubija Ewro-Mediterranja (il-Proċess ta’ Barċellona) matul il-ħames snin li ġejjin. F’Komunikazzjoni lill-Kunsill u l-Parlament, il-Kummissjoni ppreżentat proposti għal progress konkret fi tliet oqsma kruċjali għall-ġejjieni tar-reġjun: l-Edukazzjoni, it-Tkabbir Ekonomiku Sostenibbli; u d-Drittijiet tal-Bniedem u d-Demokrazija. Apparti dawn l-oqsma ta’ priorità, il-programm ta’ ħidma jkopri wkoll riformi soċjali, ambjent, migrazzjoni, armi tal-qerda tal-massa u l-ġlieda kontra t-terroriżmu. Il-Programm ta’ Ħidma tal-Kummissjoni jipprovdi aġenda għad-diskussjoni dwar it-tmexxija ’l quddiem tal-Proċess ta’ Barċellona fl-għaxar anniversarju tiegħu, kemm fil-laqgħa tal-Ministri Barranin tal-Ewro-Med li se ssir f’Mejju ġewwa l-Lussemburgu , kif ukoll fl-okkażjoni tal-konferenza ta’ l-anniversarju speċjali li se ssir f’Barċellona f’Novembru.

Il-Kummissarju għar-Relazzjonijiet Esterni u l-Politika Ewropea tal-Viċinat , Benita Ferrero-Waldner qalet “Il-Programm ta’ Ħidma propost illum għandu l-ingredjenti kollha biex jerġa’ jagħti s-saħħa lis-sħubija Ewro-Mediterranja hekk kif waslet biex tidħol fit-tieni deċennju tagħha. B’terz tal-popolazzjoni ta’ l-imsieħba tal-Mediterran taħt il-ħmistax-il sena, l-edukazzjoni hija kruċjali u din se tkun il-priorità prinċipali personali tiegħi.”

Edukazzjoni

It-titjib tal-kwalità ta’ l-edukazzjoni għal kulħadd, u l-assigurazzjoni u l-iżgurar ta’ aċċess aktar ġust huwa l-għan ċentrali tal-pjan ta’ ħidma. Il-Kummissjoni tipproponi li:

  • Iġġib ’il quddiem żjieda sostanzjali fl-appoġġ ta’ l-UE u l-Istati Membri għall-edukazzjoni u t-tħarriġ vokazzjonali, bil-għan li jiżdied b’mhux anqas minn 50% il-proporzjon ta’ assistenza finanzjarja ddestinata għall-edukazzjoni.
  • Tfittex impenn mill-imsieħba biex jiffissaw mira ġdida l-2015, biex sa dak iż-żmien jiġi eradikat l-illitteriżmu mir-reġjun; ikun hemm dħul tas-subien u l-bniet kollha fl-iskejjel primarji, u sseħħ l-eliminazzjoni tad-differenzi bejn is-sessi fil-livelli kollha ta’ l-edukazzjoni.
  • Tniedi skema li toffri boroż ta’ studju għal studju fl-Università ġewwa l-Ewropa, b’persentaġġ ta’ postijiet miżmuma għan-nisa.

Tkabbir Ekonomiku u Riformi

Id-Dikjarazzjoni ta’ Barċellona ffissat il-mira li toħloq Ftehim ta’ Kummerċ Ħieles sa l-2010. Meta fadal biss ħames snin, azzjoni hija meħtieġa biex din l-ambizzjoni ssir realtà. L-approfondiment ta’ l-integrazzjoni ekonomika bejn l-UE u l-pajjiżi tal-Mediterran, permezz ta' liberalizzazzjoni tal-kummerċ fl-agrikoltura u s-servizzi, konverġenza tar-regolamenti u investimenti kbar, se jkunu l-għanijiet prinċipali fis-snin li ġejjin. In parallel, south-south regional integration needs to be accelerated. The Commission proposes:

  • Għandu jkun hemm qbil dwar programm għall-ħolqien ta’ Żona ta’ Kummerċ Ħieles sa l-2010.
  • L-imsieħba tal-Ewro-Med iridu jniedu negożjati reġjonali fuq bażi volontarja dwar il-liberalizzazzjoni tas-servizzi u d-dritt li wieħed jistabbilihom Il-Kummissjoni se tibgħat lill-Kunsill linji ta’ gwida għan-negozjar biex jibdew in-negozjati fl-2005.
  • L-imsieħba tal-Ewro-Med għandhom jaqblu fuq programm għal-liberalizzazzjoni ta’ l-agrikoltura. Il-Kummissjoni se tibgħat lill-Kunsill linji ta’ gwida għan-negozjar biex jibdew in-negozjati fl-2005.

Drittijiet tal-Bniedem u Demokrazija

Ir-riforma politika hija l-mezz li bih tinkiseb sigurtà u stabbilità sostenibbli. Il-Kummissjoni tfittex punt fokali ġdid fi ħdan is-sħubija Ewro-Med dwar kwistjonijiet bħall-ħarsien tad-drittijiet tal-bniedem, l-għoti ta’ setgħat effettivi lin-nisa, tisħiħ tad-demokrazija, pluraliżmu u ġudikatura indipendenti. Il-Kummissjoni tipproponi kooperazzjoni akbar f’dawn l-oqsma, li jinkludu:

  • Għodda ġdida għad-Demokrazija biex tkun ta’ appoġġ għall-imsieħba li juru impenn ċar għar-riforma politika

TIMELINE FOR KEY INITIATIVES

2005
  • The Euro-Med partners should launch regional negotiations, on a voluntary basis, on the liberalisation of services and establishment. The Commission will submit negotiating guidelines for approval by the Council to this effect.
  • Euro-Mediterranean partners should agree a road map for agricultural liberalisation, including processed agricultural products and fisheries. The Commission will submit negotiating guidelines for approval by the Council to start negotiations with partner countries.
  • The Pan-Euro-Med Protocol of Origin will be progressively implemented throughout the Euromed, from 2005.

  • Organise a workshop on Weapons of Mass Destruction 2005.

  • A Euro-Mediterranean Transport Ministerial should take place before the end of the year with the participation of the EIB, to endorse a regional transport infrastructure network interconnected to the Trans-European Transport Network, and agree on the priorities of the Euro-Mediterranean Transport cooperation for the next few years.
  • A Ministerial Conference on economic and financial affairs will take place in Rabat.
2006
  • A Euro-Mediterranean Conference will be held, prepared at sub-regional level, on human rights and democratisation.

  • Approximation work in the field of technical legislation, standards and certification procedures should have intensified so as to pave the way for negotiations on conformity assessment agreements (ACAAs).

  • A Euro-Mediterranean Conference on gender equality with the participation of government representatives and civil society and social partners should take place. The Conference should concentrate on the comparison of best practices within the region to raise the role of women in society and their contribution to human development.

  • Adoption of a timetable with concrete measures with the objective of a thorough de-pollution of the Mediterranean by 2020.

  • In the light of the FEMIP experience, and following consultations with partner countries, the Commission will assess before the end of the year the possibility of establishing a Euro-Mediterranean Development Bank.
  • The Commission will launch a Scholarship scheme for university students either within the existing Erasmus Mundus programme or by reinforcing mobility activities inside the Tempus scheme.

  • A Euro–Mediterranean Energy Ministerial should take place in view of progressing sub-regional energy integration markets and infrastructures

2007
  • Euro-Mediterranean Partners should reach agreement before the end of the year on a code of conduct on measures to fight terrorism.

  • A Euro-Mediterranean conference of Justice and Home Affairs Ministers, with the participation of local authorities, should be held to discuss management of migratory flows and social integration.

  • A regional programme on rural development and optimisation of quality production should be identified for implementation.

  • After consultation with partner countries, the percentage devoted to bilateral cooperation in the education sector should be increased by 50% of the national and regional indicative programmes.
  • The Democracy Facility will enter into force.
2010
  • All South-South free trade agreements should be concluded and implemented by the end of the year, including for services and establishment as well as agriculture.

  • Completion of Euro-Mediterranean electricity and gas markets and infrastructure interconnections
