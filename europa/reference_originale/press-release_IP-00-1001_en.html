IP/00/1001

Brussels, 13 September 2000

Commission clears Unión Fenosa stake in Spain's Cable i Televisió de Catalunya (Menta)

The European Commission has granted regulatory clearance to the acquisition by Union Fenosa Inversiones SA of a stake in Menta therefore gaining joint control of the Catalan cable operator together with Telecom Italia and Endesa Telecomunicaciones. The transaction will not entail any additional overlap among the parties' activities and, therefore, does not raise competition concerns.

MENTA is a cable operator active in Catalonia (Spain) which provides services related to cable TV systems, video-communications, voice and data transmission and any other services linked to cable telecommunications.

Before the operation MENTA was jointly controlled by Telecom Italia, Endesa Telecomunicaciones S.A., and a group of shareholders including Spain Telecommunications Limited s.a.r.l. (Spaincom), Tisa Cable S.L. and Cableuropa. The later three shareholders will sell their 40-percent controlling stake in Menta to the other partners plus Union Fenosa.

Following the operation MENTA will be jointly controlled by Telecom Italia, Endesa Telecomunicaciones S.A. and Unión Fenosa Inversiones S.A. through their joint holding company AUNA.
